Meeting My Past Self

A funny thing happened to me today. I switched on my computer, and I was rummaging through the poopsmear of files on my computer, and I opened a document containing an essay I wrote to a graduate student. The contents of the essay were basically a plea to get this poor reader to change my grade. But the way I did it was very weird and a wee bit on the bold side—I invoked Hurricane Sandy.

That’s right—talked about one of the most dangerous and serious natural events ever to occur in United States history alongside an explanation into why my B+ should be changed to an A+. The gall! The heresy! One would think I was a member of the Spartan army, hastily disguised as an Athenian in the heat of the battle, before I stripped off my vile, poop-encrusted mask and thrust my blade into an unsuspecting graduate’s student’s heart. The heart, mind you, was already weak from the emotional imagery I evoked of sinking homes and abandoned families. It was if I was sliding a serrated blade into a soft slab of butter, milking the graduate student’s empathy for all it’s grade-inflationary worth. And by god I succeeded in my quest…while defaming millions of hurricane-affected tri-state area families in the process. That’s the New York-New Jersey-Connecticut tri-state area for all you humans unfamiliar with the naming conventions of New Yorkers. Which is probably most of you. Although they certainly don’t realize that.

I guess what I’m trying to say is that I would never have remembered writing that essay if it wasn’t for the fact I was dicking around my computer. That part of me would literally be dead and never even visited once in the cemetery. But it was almost like stumbling upon a gravestone and meeting an old friend—I was introduced to the very immature thought-patterns of my 2012 self, while marveling at the fact that I was able to craft a quasi-legitimate argument that my grade should be changed.

I also want to get out of the way that I am not that much of a jerk for bringing up Hurricane Sandy in my essay. Part of the ‘protocol’ for changing grades is that the graduate student wanted us to write about something else besides the reason for the change in the paper, so she wouldn’t be bored to death while slogging through our argument. Apparently graduate students have a read a lot of snoozer papers, although this is probably just a long-standing rumor whose validity will never be proven. Anyway, I heeded the grad student’s advice and incorporated a very gripping commentary into Hurricane Sandy in my paper, which made the thing a very enjoyable read these years later.

And to answer your question, yes—the grade was changed. I think. I hope. I’d like to think my previous self knew what he was doing. But that’s probably not the case. Because my present self certainly doesn’t.
